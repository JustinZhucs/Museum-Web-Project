The British Museum

1. Rosetta Stone

A giant slab inscribed with three versions of a decree issued at Memphis, Egypt in 196 BC. After it was discovered in 1799, this stone turned out to be one of the key factors in deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs.

2. SUTTON HOO MASK

The Sutton Hoo Mask is an ornate anglo-saxon helmet found during the Burial of the Sutton Hoo ship in 1939. It’s elaborate decoration may have given it an auxiliary function similar to that of a crown.

3. EGYPTIAN MUMMIES

Most of the mummies in the British Museum are completely wrapped and painted, but some have been opened and exposed to the air. In addition to adult mummies, there are also mummies of children and cats.

4. ENLIGHTENMENT GALLERY

Dedicated to the 18th-century Enlightenment era, this beautiful gallery was once known as the King’s Library. It was built between 1823 and 1827 to house over 60,000 books collected by King George III.
